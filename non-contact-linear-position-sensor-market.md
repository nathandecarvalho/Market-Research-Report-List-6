<p><p>The Non-contact Linear Position Sensor market is divided into two primary types based on output signals: Analog and Digital. Analog output sensors provide continuous voltage or current signals proportional to the position, offering high resolution and real-time data, making them suitable for applications requiring smooth and precise measurements. In contrast, Digital output sensors deliver discrete signals, often in binary form, which are ideal for applications needing precise positioning or limit detection, enhancing compatibility with digital systems and microcontrollers.</p></p>
