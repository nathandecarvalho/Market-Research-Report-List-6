<p><p>Three-line breeding systems in agriculture involve two types of markets: Type I and Type II. Type I markets focus on elite hybrids produced by crossing specific parent lines, ensuring high performance and uniformity. These hybrids cater to commercial farmers seeking optimal yields. Type II markets, on the other hand, emphasize the development of open-pollinated varieties, allowing farmers to save seeds while promoting genetic diversity. This approach suits resource-limited farmers who may prioritize sustainability over maximum yield.</p></p>
