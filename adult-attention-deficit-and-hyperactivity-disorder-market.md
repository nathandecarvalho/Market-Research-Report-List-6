<p><p>The Adult Attention Deficit and Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) market is primarily divided into two categories: stimulant and non-stimulant medications. Stimulant medications, such as amphetamines and methylphenidate, are the most commonly prescribed and work by enhancing the brain's dopamine and norepinephrine levels to improve focus and reduce impulsivity. Non-stimulant medications, like atomoxetine and guanfacine, offer alternatives for individuals who may not respond well to stimulants or prefer a lower risk of abuse. Both categories aim to effectively manage ADHD symptoms in adults.</p></p>
