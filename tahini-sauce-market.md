<p><p>The tahini sauce market is primarily divided into two types: hulled tahini and unhulled tahini. Hulled tahini is made from sesame seeds with their outer hulls removed, resulting in a smoother texture and milder flavor, making it popular for culinary applications. In contrast, unhulled tahini retains the seed hulls, giving it a nuttier taste and higher nutritional value due to increased fiber and calcium content. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences and health trends, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
