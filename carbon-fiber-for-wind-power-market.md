<p><p>The carbon fiber for wind power market includes several types based on raw materials. PAN-based carbon fiber, derived from polyacrylonitrile, offers high tensile strength and lightweight properties, making it ideal for turbine blades. Viscose-based carbon fiber, produced from regenerated cellulose, provides good mechanical properties but is less common. Pitch-based carbon fiber, which comes from petroleum or coal tar pitch, features excellent thermal stability. Other market types may include specialty fibers tailored for specific applications in wind energy production.</p></p>
