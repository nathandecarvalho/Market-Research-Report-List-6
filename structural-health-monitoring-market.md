<p><p>Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) market can be broadly categorized into wired and wireless systems. Wired SHM uses physical cables for data transmission, providing reliable connectivity and high data integrity, ideal for permanent installations. However, installation can be complex and costly. In contrast, wireless SHM utilizes sensors that transmit data via wireless networks, offering flexibility and easier deployment, especially in remote or challenging environments. Both types are crucial for assessing the integrity and safety of structures, ensuring timely maintenance and prevention of failures.</p></p>
